## Script & Visual Steps

### INTRO (0:00 - 0:15)
**[Screen: Show signature examples]**

> "Hi! This quick guide will show you how to install your new professional email signature in Microsoft Outlook. It takes about 2 minutes. Let's get started!"

---

### STEP 1: OPEN THE SIGNATURE FILE (0:15 - 0:35)
**[Screen: Windows Explorer showing signatures folder]**

> "First, locate your signature HTML file. It will be named something like 'signature-john-doe.html'."

**[Screen: Right-click file ‚Üí Open with ‚Üí Chrome/Edge]**

> "Right-click the file and open it with your web browser - Chrome, Edge, or Firefox work great."

**[Screen: Signature displays in browser]**

> "The signature will open in your browser. Don't worry if it looks slightly different than the final result - that's normal."

---

### STEP 2: COPY THE SIGNATURE (0:35 - 0:50)
**[Screen: Browser with signature open]**

> "Now, select all the content. You can do this by clicking anywhere in the browser, then pressing Ctrl+A on Windows, or Command+A on Mac."

**[Screen: Content highlighted]**

> "Everything should be highlighted in blue. Now copy it with Ctrl+C, or Command+C on Mac."

---

### STEP 3: OPEN OUTLOOK SIGNATURE SETTINGS (0:50 - 1:20)
**[Screen: Outlook application]**

> "Open Microsoft Outlook. Click on 'File' in the top left corner."

**[Screen: File menu open]**

> "Then click 'Options' at the bottom of the menu."

**[Screen: Outlook Options dialog]**

> "In the Outlook Options window, click 'Mail' on the left side."

**[Screen: Mail options with Signatures button visible]**

> "Now click the 'Signatures' button in the middle of the screen."

---

### STEP 4: CREATE NEW SIGNATURE (1:20 - 2:00)
**[Screen: Signatures and Stationery dialog]**

> "In the Signatures window, click the 'New' button to create a new signature."

**[Screen: Name dialog box]**

> "Give your signature a name - something simple like 'John Doe Work' or just your name."

**[Screen: Empty signature editor box]**

> "Click in the large signature editor box below."

**[Screen: Paste action, Ctrl+V shown]**

> "Now paste your signature by pressing Ctrl+V, or Command+V on Mac."

**[Screen: Signature appears in editor]**

> "Your signature should appear with all the formatting, images, and links intact!"

---

### STEP 5: SET DEFAULT SIGNATURE (2:00 - 2:30)
**[Screen: Signature dropdown menus]**

> "Almost done! Now tell Outlook when to use this signature."

**[Screen: Point to 'New messages' dropdown]**

> "In the 'Choose default signature' section, select your email account from the dropdown."

**[Screen: Select signature from 'New messages' dropdown]**

> "Then select your signature from the 'New messages' dropdown. This adds it to every new email automatically."

**[Screen: Select signature from 'Replies/forwards' dropdown]**

> "You can also add it to 'Replies/forwards' if you want, though many people prefer to leave this blank."

---

### STEP 6: SAVE AND TEST (2:30 - 3:00)
**[Screen: Click OK buttons]**

> "Click 'OK' in the Signatures window, then 'OK' again in the Options window."

**[Screen: Create new email in Outlook]**

> "Let's test it! Click 'New Email' to create a message."

**[Screen: Email compose window with signature at bottom]**

> "Perfect! Your signature appears at the bottom of the email, ready to go."

---

### TESTING (3:00 - 3:30)
**[Screen: Click on links in signature]**

> "Before you start using it, test the links. Click your email address - it should open a new email. Click your phone number - it should offer to dial. Click LinkedIn - it should open your company page."

**[Screen: Send test email]**

> "Send yourself a test email to make sure everything looks good. Check it on your phone too!"

---

### TROUBLESHOOTING (3:30 - 3:50)
**[Screen: Common issues overlay]**

> "If images aren't showing, they may not be hosted online yet. Contact your IT department for help."

> "If links aren't clickable, try pasting again or use Internet Explorer/Edge to open the HTML file instead."

---

### OUTRO (3:50 - 4:00)
**[Screen: Show all 5 signature examples side by side]**

> "That's it! You now have a professional email signature in Outlook. If you need help, check the README file or contact IT support. Happy emailing!"

## üéØ Key Points to Emphasize

1. Use a web browser (not Word or text editor)
2. Copy the WHOLE signature (Ctrl+A then Ctrl+C)
3. Paste directly into Outlook's signature editor
4. Set as default for new messages
5. Always send a test email
6. Check on mobile devices too
